SectionHeadingWhat It SaysAmendment
Sec 16(1)Basic ITC entitlementEvery registered person is entitled to take credit of input tax charged on any supply of goods or services used in the course of furtherance of business — subject to conditions in Section 16(2) to 16(4).Original 2017
Sec 16(2)(a)Valid tax invoiceITC is available only if the registered person holds a valid tax invoice or debit note issued by a registered supplier, or such other tax paying document as may be prescribed.Original 2017
Sec 16(2)(aa)GSTR-2B conditionThe details of invoice or debit note referred to in Section 16(2)(a) have been furnished by the supplier in their GSTR-1 and such details are communicated to the recipient in GSTR-2B. Inserted w.e.f. 01.01.2022Finance Act 2021
Sec 16(2)(b)Receipt of goods/servicesThe registered person has received the goods or services or both. Where goods are delivered to a third party on the direction of the registered person, it shall be deemed that the registered person has received the goods.Original 2017
Sec 16(2)(c)Tax paid to governmentThe tax charged in respect of the supply has been actually paid to the Government — either in cash or through utilisation of input tax credit admissible in respect of the supply. This is the "supplier default" condition — the most litigated in India.Original 2017
Sec 16(2)(d)Return filing conditionThe registered person has furnished the return under Section 39 (GSTR-3B). Failure to file return extinguishes the right to claim ITC for that period.Original 2017
Sec 16(3)No double benefitWhere a registered person has claimed depreciation on the cost of capital goods including the GST component under the Income Tax Act 1961, ITC on such goods shall not be allowed.Original 2017
Sec 16(4)Time limit — THE CRITICAL PROVISIONA registered person shall NOT be entitled to take input tax credit in respect of any invoice or debit note after: (a) the due date of furnishing the return under Section 39 for the month of September following the end of financial year, OR (b) the date of furnishing the annual return — whichever is earlier. This is the most commonly litigated ITC denial provision in India.Original 2017 — September amended to November
Sec 16(5)Retrospective relief — Finance Act 2024Notwithstanding Section 16(4), where the registered person has filed their GSTR-3B on or before 30th November 2021 for the financial years 2017-18, 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21, the ITC in respect of any invoice or debit note shall be deemed to have been taken within the time limit prescribed under Section 16(4). Inserted retrospectively w.e.f. 01.07.2017 by Finance Act 2024Finance (No.2) Act 2024 — S.118
Sec 16(6)ITC after cancellation/restorationWhere registration is cancelled and subsequently restored, the registered person shall be entitled to take ITC in respect of invoices covering the period between cancellation and restoration, subject to conditions and restrictions. Inserted by Finance Act 2024Finance (No.2) Act 2024
Sec 17(5)Blocked creditsNotwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1) of Section 16 and sub-section (1) of Section 18, input tax credit shall NOT be available in respect of: motor vehicles (subject to exceptions), food and beverages, outdoor catering, beauty treatment, health services, cosmetic surgery, membership of clubs, travel benefits to employees, works contract services for immovable property (subject to exceptions), goods or services for personal consumption, and goods lost, stolen, destroyed or written off.Amended 2018, 2019
Sec 18Availability of credit in special circumstancesCovers ITC entitlement on: conversion from composition to regular scheme, obtaining registration for first time, switchover from exempt to taxable supplies, and credit on capital goods. Also covers reversal of ITC on supply becoming exempt or where registration is cancelled.Original 2017
Rule 36(4)Provisional ITC restriction — CGST RulesInput tax credit to be availed by a registered person in respect of invoices or debit notes, the details of which have not been uploaded by the suppliers, shall not exceed 20% of the eligible credit available in respect of invoices the details of which have been uploaded. This percentage was 10% before 01.01.2022. From 01.01.2022, NO provisional ITC is available — only GSTR-2B reflected amounts are eligible. Inserted 09.10.2019 — amended 29.12.2021Notf 49/2019, Notf 40/2021
Sec 73Show Cause Notice — non-fraud casesWhere it appears to the proper officer that any tax has not been paid or short paid or erroneously refunded, or where ITC has been wrongly availed or utilised — the proper officer shall serve a Show Cause Notice before making any demand. The SCN is mandatory. Time limit: SCN to be issued within 3 years of the due date of filing the annual return for the year to which the demand relates. Order to be passed within 3 years of the SCN. Demand without SCN is void ab initio.Original 2017 — Sec 128A inserted 2024
Sec 75(4)Mandatory personal hearingAn opportunity of hearing shall be granted where a request is received in writing from the person to whom notice has been issued to show cause under Section 73. Denial of personal hearing when requested is a procedural defect that independently renders the order liable to be set aside.Original 2017
Sec 128AWaiver of interest/penalty — special casesThe Government may, by notification, waive payment of interest under Section 50 or penalty under Section 73 in respect of demands for the period July 2017 to March 2020, subject to specified conditions. Inserted to provide relief for early GST period compliance issues. Inserted by Finance Act 2024Finance (No.2) Act 2024

Each state has enacted its own GST Rules mirroring the CGST Rules. Rule 36(4) applies identically under all State GST Rules. State-specific variations exist primarily in procedural rules for hearings, service of notices, and local forms. For ITC purposes, the substantive rules are identical across all states.
